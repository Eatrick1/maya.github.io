Our Farm Activities

"Maya Farm is not one farm — it is six enterprises working as one living system. Every activity feeds the next, reducing waste, building soil, and multiplying value."

01

Coffee Farming

At the heart of Maya Farm lies our flagship enterprise — arabica and robusta coffee cultivation across 45 acres of shade-grown plantation. Uganda's equatorial climate and the rich red-clay soils of Wakiso create a perfect terroir for producing beans of exceptional cup quality.

We grow primarily Arabica SL-14 and SL-28 varieties — prized by specialty roasters globally — alongside Robusta blocks for blending and domestic market supply. Our trees are intercropped with banana, Grevillea shade trees and nitrogen-fixing legumes, building a biodiverse microecosystem that reduces pest pressure naturally.

45 acres of shade-grown Arabica and Robusta plantation

Certified specialty-grade: 84+ SCA cupping scores achieved

Direct trade relationships with European roasters

Annual yield: 12–15 metric tonnes green bean equivalent

02

Coffee Processing

Value addition is central to our business model. Rather than selling raw cherry at farmgate prices, Maya Farm operates its own wet and dry processing mill — one of the few farm-level operations in Wakiso with this capability.

We offer washed, natural, and honey-process methods, allowing buyers to commission experimental micro-lots for the specialty market. Our solar-powered drying beds and raised African drying tables meet international food-safety standards.

On-site wet mill with ecological waste-water management

Washed, natural and honey process all available

Solar-powered drying infrastructure — zero fossil fuel

Micro-lot traceability from tree to export bag

03

Banana Plantation

Uganda's staple crop finds a premium expression at Maya Farm. Our 35-acre banana plantation covers East African highland cooking bananas (matooke), as well as dessert varieties including Gros Michel and Cavendish for fresh-market and export.

Tissue-culture planting material ensures disease-free, high-yielding stands. Mature banana leaves and pseudo-stems are mulched back into the soil, returning potassium and organic matter — a key pillar of our zero-waste farming system.

35 acres of tissue-culture matooke and dessert varieties

Primary supply channel to Kampala wholesale markets

Banana flour processing line for value-added export product

Intercropped with coffee for shade and soil benefit

04

Free-Range Poultry

Maya Farm raises indigenous Ugandan Kienyeji chicken alongside improved Kuroiler and Rainbow Rooster breeds — all managed under free-range, non-antibiotic protocols. Our birds forage on naturally grown grasses, insects and supplementary organic feed formulated on the farm.

Eggs and dressed chicken are sold to premium hotels, restaurants and households in Kampala who pay a certified premium for traceable, antibiotic-free poultry products. Poultry manure is composted and returned to our coffee and banana blocks.

3,500 free-range birds across indigenous and hybrid breeds

Zero antibiotic, zero hormone — certified clean protocol

Supplying premium hotels and restaurants in Kampala

Manure converted to compost powering crop enterprise

05

Sustainable Piggery

Our piggery operates a closed herd of Large White and Landrace crossbreeds — selected for their adaptability to tropical conditions and high feed conversion efficiency. Pigs are fed on a combination of on-farm banana stems, sweet potato vines, and balanced supplementary rations.

The biogas plant integrated with our piggery waste management converts slurry into cooking gas for farm workers' quarters, and the effluent is channelled into our fish ponds, creating a multi-loop nutrient cycle that dramatically reduces external input costs.

180+ pigs including sows, boars and growing stock

Biogas plant converting manure to clean cooking energy

Premium pork supply to Kampala butcheries and caterers

Effluent nutrient cycle linked to fish pond production

06

Vegetables & Apiary

Seasonal organic vegetables — including kale, tomatoes, capsicum, onions, and sukuma wiki — are grown in rotation on 10 acres of irrigated market-garden beds, serving Kampala supermarkets, hotels and direct subscription customers.

Thirty Langstroth beehives positioned throughout the coffee and banana plots provide pollination services that measurably increase our crop yields, while producing 400+ kg of raw artisanal honey per season — bottled and sold under the Maya Farm brand.

10 acres rotational vegetable production under drip irrigation

30 Langstroth hives producing 400+ kg honey per season

Bees provide pollination service increasing crop yields 18%

Branded Maya Farm Honey sold in Kampala premium retail
